SPRING 2017 AT THE ADLER PLANETARIUM
THE "YEAR OF THE ECLIPSE" AT THE ADLER

As we prepare to celebrate the eclipse, we’ll be raising awareness, drumming up excitement, and giving people all the tools they need to make the most of this rare celestial event. Our goal is All Eyes on the Sky on August 21, 2017. Efforts will include:

  • Launching a temporary exhibition, Chasing Eclipses, in spring 2017.
  • Working with schools to encourage students to #LookUp wherever they may be.
  • Offering an Eclipse Party pack and training so that groups around Chicagoland can host their own solar eclipse viewing parties
ALL EYES ON THE SKY EVENT

On August 21, 2017, at 11:54am, the Moon will start to block Chicago’s view of the Sun. At the Adler, we’ll be ready to celebrate all things solar with free outdoor activities, safe viewing of the Sun, music, solar cars, solar cooking demonstrations, and so much more!

We’ll also have a team of Adler astronomers and educators stationed downstate in Carbondale, Illinois—directly in the path of totality—to lead activities, answer questions, and experience the event with the public. The crowd is likely to be very large and to include eclipse-chasers from around the world!
